About TruthScan

Fighting misinformation in India-Pakistan news

Why I Made This Tool

Misinformation in India-Pakistan news can stir up trouble and even cause harm. I created TruthScan to give people a quick way to check if a news article seems legit.

My tool uses a mix of rule-based checks and pattern spotting to flag signs of fake news. It’s not perfect, but it’s a solid starting point for figuring out if you need to dig deeper.

Heads-Up

This is an automated tool, so don’t rely on it alone. Always double-check with trusted sources.

My Approach

How I built the checking process

Language Check

I look for emotional, overblown, or clickbaity wording common in fake news. Real news usually keeps it calm and measured.

Source Verification

Good news names specific sources, experts, or groups. I check for those and see if they’re trustworthy.

Balance Check

I look for fair reporting that shows both sides, especially in India-Pakistan news where bias can sneak in.

Context Check

My tool weighs the content against historical and political context to catch misrepresentations of tricky regional issues.
